Which Microsoft does what?
The very serious quiz™

15 questions to break the ice, bust a few myths, and prove that "there is more than one right answer" is itself the right answer. We close with the BXT framework so you walk out able to score your own use cases.

🧊 Round 1 · Icebreakers (warm up the room)

Q1

A caseworker asks: "Which Microsoft tool should I open?" What's the most honest answer?

Reveal

◆ It depends. Really. A good platform is one that fits the task. Today we'll meet 8 capabilities — not 1 hammer for every nail.

Q2

"One Microsoft Ecosystem" means…

Reveal

● Connected platforms. Dynamics 365 captures cases, Fabric collects the data, Foundry/Copilot Studio build the agents, M365 surfaces it in Word/Excel/Teams. Nobody needs to go "all-in" on one thing.

🏗️ Round 2 · Right platform for the right job

Q3 Business Platform

A new victim case comes in via the website. Where should it ideally land?

Reveal

● Dynamics 365. Structured case, audit trail, workflows, SLAs. Excel might survive day 1; it won't survive day 365.

Q4 Data & Analytics

The board wants to know: "How many cases per district per quarter?" Where do you build that?

Reveal

▲ Power BI on Fabric. Live, governed, repeatable. Plot twist: ▲ and ● can both be right — start small with Excel, scale up to Fabric once it gets repetitive. It's not betrayal.

Q5 AI Agents

You want a chatbot that only answers questions about Slachtofferhulp procedures (and not about the Champions League). What do you pick?

Reveal

◆ Copilot Studio or Foundry. Both are great — pick based on skill and complexity. Quick PoC? Copilot Studio. Custom RAG + evals + grounding on Fabric? Foundry. Both ground on your data — no hallucinated football trivia.

Q6 Productivity

A caseworker wants AI to summarize a long report inside Word. Best place to do this?

Reveal

◆ M365 Copilot. Data stays inside your tenant + governance. Option ■ is genuinely dangerous with victim data — privacy by design starts with tool choice.

Q7 Trust & Safety

An AI agent is about to say something to a victim. What sits in between to prevent "accidents"?

Reveal

◆ Content Safety + Responsible AI. Moderation, hate/violence/self-harm filters, prompt-injection protection. For a victim support organization this is non-negotiable.

Q8 Content Understanding

A pile of scanned police reports (PDFs + audio recordings of intakes). What extracts structured data from them?

Reveal

◆ Content Understanding. One service for documents, audio AND video. Output lands neatly in Fabric for analysis.

🔀 Round 3 · Mythbusters

Q9

True or False: "You must pick one Microsoft platform and stick with it forever."

Reveal

◆ False. This is the whole point of today. Copilot Studio + Foundry can coexist. Fabric and Excel too. Nobody wins a prize for being a purist.

Q10

Which statement about Microsoft Fabric vs Power BI is correct?

Reveal

◆. Fabric is the broader data platform; Power BI is the visualization layer on top. You can absolutely keep using Power BI on its own.

Q11

"Low-code" agents in Copilot Studio means…

Reveal

◆. Low-code = start faster with domain knowledge in the room. For complex agents, evals and custom RAG you graduate to Foundry. Both live side by side.

🎯 Round 4 · BXT — How do you pick a good use case?

Q12

What does BXT stand for in the Microsoft envisioning framework?

Reveal

◆ Business · Experience · Technology. Three lenses to score any AI use case honestly BEFORE you start building.

Q13

Under "Business" you mostly check…

Reveal

◆. Without a sponsor, measurable value and a realistic timeline → straight to the pilot graveyard.

Q14

Under "Experience" the focus is mostly on…

Reveal

◆. A beautiful product with no demand = expensive hobby project. Start at the caseworker, not at the tech.

Q15

Two use cases: (A) Volunteer matcher bot, (B) Predictive case prioritization. Both score high on Business + Experience. How do you choose?

Reveal

◆. The T in BXT is the tiebreaker. High feasibility + low risk → start there. The other one goes on the roadmap, not in the bin.

🏁 Wrap-up — three things to remember

1. One ecosystem, many tools.

Dynamics 365 · Microsoft 365 · Fabric · Foundry · Copilot Studio · M365 Copilot · Content Understanding · Content Safety. They talk to each other — pick per problem, not per religion.

2. More than one answer is allowed.

Copilot Studio and Foundry. Excel and Fabric. Start small, scale once value is proven. No holy wars required.

3. BXT before you build.

Business (sponsor, value, time) · Experience (user demand, adoption) · Technology (data, feasibility, risk). Score first, build second. Saves time, money and frustration.
